How long does a Chicago move take?

Sparkle Moving Team Published [date] 5 min read
Share

Most local moves in Chicago take between two and ten hours, depending on the size of the home and the specifics of the addresses on each end. That’s the short answer.

Here’s the longer one.

The biggest factor: home size

Crew size is matched to home size, and crew size determines pace.

Home size Typical crew Typical hours
Studio / 1-bedroom 2 movers 2–4 hours
2-bedroom 3 movers 4–6 hours
3+ bedroom 4 movers 6–10 hours

These are local moves, address to address, no storage stop. Long-distance moves don’t run on a clock — they have a delivery window instead. See Long Distance Moving for how those work.

What changes the time

Three things, mostly:

1. How packed the home is.

A 2-bedroom apartment with everything in boxes ready to go loads faster than a 2-bedroom with last-minute packing happening as the crew works.

2. Building access.

Walk-up to walk-up is faster than freight-elevator-with-COI to freight-elevator-with-COI. Most Chicago high-rises require 30–60 minutes for elevator booking and dock check-in on each end.

3. Distance between addresses.

A move within Lincoln Park is faster than one from Evanston to Hyde Park. Drive time between addresses is on the clock.

What’s not on the clock

The drive from our warehouse to your pickup, and from your destination back to our warehouse — those are covered by the one-time travel fee, not the hourly rate. Loading is when the clock starts; final placement is when it stops.

Full breakdown on the Pricing page.

Short estimates that overrun aren’t bait — they’re usually missing information.

A real example

A 2-bedroom condo in Lakeview moving to a 2-bedroom apartment in Wicker Park, both with elevator buildings, COIs filed in advance, average packing:

  • 30 minutes: walkthrough, floor protection, dolly setup
  • 2 hours: load
  • 30 minutes: drive
  • 2 hours: unload and placement
  • 30 minutes: walkthrough and reassemble beds

Total: 5.5 hours, 3 movers, $930 + $169 travel = $1,099.

That’s a typical day. Yours might run an hour shorter or an hour longer. The quote shows the range, the invoice shows actual time worked.

When moves run long

Most overruns come from one of these:

  • Packing not finished when the crew arrives
  • Surprise items not on the inventory (extra closet, basement storage, garage)
  • Building access issues (elevator already booked, dock unavailable)
  • Long-carry situations (parking blocked, no front-door access)

The best way to keep your move on schedule is to walk through your inventory honestly during the quote.
