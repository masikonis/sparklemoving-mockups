What happens next

  1. You submit. Takes about 60 seconds.
  2. We review. A real person looks at your ZIPs, dates, and details. Usually within a few hours during business hours.
  3. You get a breakdown. For local moves: crew size, hourly rate, hour range, total estimate. For long-distance moves: a binding quote with weight allowance and delivery window.

No back-and-forth required if the basics are clear. If anything’s missing or unusual, we send one or two follow-up questions before quoting.

What you won’t get

  • A deposit request — we don’t take any
  • Spam emails — your address is used only for the quote
  • A pressure call — we send the breakdown by email; the call is on you
  • A bait-and-switch — the quote is the basis for the invoice, not a starting point

How accurate is the quote?

For local moves, the estimate is an hour range. The final invoice reflects actual time worked, billed at the hourly rate from the quote. No overtime multiplier. If the crew finishes early, you’re billed for actual hours only.

For long-distance moves, the quote is binding — total cost, weight allowance, and delivery window all locked in. Doesn’t change between quote and delivery.

FAQ

Do I need to know my exact moving date?

No. Approximate is fine. We can adjust the date once your plans are confirmed, with seven days notice or more.

What if my dates change after I book?

Seven days notice or more: no fee. Less than seven days: depends on crew availability.

Do you call me after I submit?

Only if the request is missing something or the move is unusual. Otherwise the quote comes by email.

How long does the quote take to arrive?

Usually a few hours during business hours. Always within one business day.

Do you take weekend or holiday bookings?

Yes. Same hourly rate every day of the week. No weekend or holiday surcharge.

Can I get a binding quote for a local move?

Local moves are billed hourly because the time required varies with access, packing, and other on-site factors. The estimate is an hour range, billed at the rate in the quote. The final invoice can come in below the range if the crew finishes early.

My move is unusual (piano, two-leg, freight elevator, COI). Should I still use this form?

Yes. Submit the basics here and we’ll follow up with the specific questions for your situation.
