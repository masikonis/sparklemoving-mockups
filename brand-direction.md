# Brand Direction: Sparkle Moving & Storage

## Identity

### Emotional anchor

The calm certainty that this will go smoothly. Not because someone promised it would, but because everything is visibly organized and honestly priced.

**Three words:** Trustworthy. Organized. Easy.

### The test

**Does this feel like a company that would show you exactly what your move costs before you ask?**

If it feels like it's trying to impress, reassure, entertain, or hide behind a quote form, it fails. The right direction feels structured, direct, and transparent. A company that shows you the math, not one that asks you to trust the brand.

### Design principles

**1. Structure over atmosphere.**
Information comes first. Layout and hierarchy do the work that mood photography and emotional headlines do for other brands. The site should feel organized before it feels anything else.

**2. Show the math.**
Transparency means visible numbers, not promises. Every competitor says "transparent pricing" but hides prices behind a quote form. Sparkle publishes real prices. The brand treats pricing as content, not as a conversion gate.

**3. Earned trust, not claimed trust.**
Trust comes from verifiable specifics (years of operation, volume of work, credentials), not superlatives. The brand proves credibility through data and structure, never through adjectives.

**4. Frictionless, not effortless.**
Every unnecessary step between a visitor and a quote gets removed. But the brand never pretends moving itself is easy. Sparkle removes process friction. It doesn't sell a fantasy.

**5. Detail that rewards attention.**
Sophistication lives in restraint and clever details, not volume. The logo's handtruck integrated as a letterform is the template: a detail that rewards a second look without demanding a first one.

## Anti-patterns

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Empty superlatives ("Number one choice," "World-class experience") | Claims without evidence. The brand earns credibility through specifics. |
| Fake reassurance ("Dedicated to excellence," "Sit back and relax") | Tells people to feel something instead of giving them a reason to. |
| Startup aesthetic | Too experimental and unproven for a company with 14 years of moves behind it. |
| Cheap local service look | Contradicts the mid-market positioning and the quality the brand delivers. |
| Corporate feel | Stiff, impersonal, templated. The brand is direct and human, not institutional. |
| Playful brand | Moving is stressful. The response is calm competence, not entertainment. |
| Flashy or trendy design | Attention-seeking undermines the restraint at the brand's core. |
| Fake pricing transparency | Saying "transparent pricing" while hiding prices behind a quote form. If the numbers aren't visible, it's not transparency. |
| Scroll-triggered animations that hide content | Content is visible by default. Nothing that makes someone wait to see information. |
| SEO-first copy | Dry, keyword-stuffed text written for search engines instead of anxious homeowners. |
| All-caps heading treatment | Franchise energy that doesn't match the brand's restrained, trustworthy positioning. |

## Voice

### Voice character

Sparkle speaks in short, direct sentences. States facts, not feelings. Positions by ruling out extremes: not the cheapest, not the most expensive; not a sales pitch, not a form letter. There's no filler, no fluff, no gimmicks. Every sentence either tells you what the company does, what it costs, or what happens next.

Underneath the directness is quiet confidence. This is a company that's done over 10,000 moves and doesn't need to convince you. It lays out the information clearly and lets the structure speak. The tone is closer to a calm professional explaining your options than a brand trying to build a relationship.

### We are / we aren't

| We are | We aren't |
|--------|-----------|
| Direct. State what we do and what it costs. | Salesy. No persuasion language, no empty promises. |
| Mid-market. Professional crews, fair pricing. | Budget or luxury. Not the cheapest option, not white-glove at a premium. |
| Organized. Everything has structure and sequence. | Chaotic or overly casual. Nothing improvised. |
| Honest about pricing. Real numbers visible. | Opaque. No "contact us for a quote" as the only path. |
| Efficient. Fast booking, no unnecessary steps. | Slow or friction-heavy. No multi-page forms for a simple question. |

### Language signals

**Sounds like us:**
- Here's what your move costs
- No hidden fees. No surprises.
- Two-bedroom apartment, local move, here's the range
- Short, declarative statements that get to the point

**Doesn't sound like us:**
- "Number one choice," "world-class experience"
- "Dedicated to excellence"
- "Sit back and relax"
- Superlatives without evidence
- Anything playful, flashy, or trying to entertain

## Directional signals

### Color

Navy is the brand's foundation: grounded and professional. White provides space and structure. Yellow/gold from the logo carries into the site as an accent only: highlights, interactive elements, moments of emphasis. The palette is restrained. Red exists in the broader brand palette but plays a secondary role to the navy/white/gold relationship established by the logo and the door sign.

The overall palette character is calm and structured. Nothing saturated enough to feel energetic, nothing muted enough to feel passive.

### Typography

Type should signal the same character as the logo: clean, geometric, structured, confident. Sturdy enough to carry authority without feeling corporate or stiff. Modern but not trendy. Nothing that dates quickly. The logo's wide-tracked, geometric letterforms set the reference point; site typography should complement that weight and clarity without mimicking it.

Not decorative. Not playful. Not the ultra-thin or display-heavy treatment of startup brands.

### Imagery

Branded photography is not available at launch; a photoshoot is planned for later in the year. The visual identity should not depend on photography. The specific approach for handling the gap belongs in visual exploration, not here.

When photography arrives, it should show real crews on real jobs. Working trucks, actual moves in progress. Never staged, never stock, never lifestyle imagery disconnected from the work.

### Motion

Functional, not decorative. Motion eases transitions and confirms actions. It doesn't create spectacle. Calm and deliberate. Nothing bouncy, nothing that makes someone wait to see content that should already be visible.
