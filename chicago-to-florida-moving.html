How a Chicago to Florida move works

1. Quote and inventory

Tell us what you’re moving. For long-distance, we take an inventory by phone or short video — what furniture, how many boxes, anything oversized. The inventory drives the binding quote.

2. Pickup

Crew arrives in Chicago on the agreed date. Loading takes a full day for most homes. Same crew loads as drives, in most cases.

3. Transit

3–5 days, depending on Florida destination. South Florida is the longer end, North Florida is the shorter end. The truck is yours alone — no co-mingled inventory, no detour to drop off another customer’s load.

4. Delivery

3-day delivery window, locked in at the time of booking. We confirm the exact day 24–48 hours ahead. Unloading takes a full day. Reassembly included.

5. Final invoice

The number on the invoice matches the binding quote. No fuel surcharge, no weight surprise, no last-minute additions.

Why this route specifically

We run it regularly. Chicago to Florida is one of our most-booked long-distance routes. That means predictable scheduling and steady availability — not a one-off “let us see if we can fit it in.”

Return runs. Trucks coming back from Florida often run the route in reverse. If you’re moving Florida to Chicago, the same dedicated-truck binding-quote model applies.

No shared loads. Some long-distance movers wait until they have enough cargo to fill a truck before they leave. That’s how three-week delivery windows happen. Our trucks leave on the date you booked, full or not.

Real delivery windows. Three-day window at booking, not “sometime in two to three weeks.” If we’d miss the window, we tell you the moment we know — not at delivery.

What’s included

  • Dedicated truck (yours alone)
  • Same crew or coordinated handoff (single point of contact the whole route)
  • Furniture wrapping (blankets for wood and upholstered, shrink wrap for mattresses)
  • Disassembly at pickup, reassembly at delivery
  • Standard liability coverage (full-value protection available — see Insurance & Licensing →)
  • Every Florida destination — no surcharge for any specific city in the state

What’s not included

  • Packing supplies, if you want us to pack (quoted with the move)
  • Specialty items like pianos, safes, or items over 400 lbs (quoted separately)
  • Storage at either end (available, see Storage Services →)

Real timeline example

A 2-bedroom move from Lincoln Park, Chicago, to Tampa, Florida:

  • Day 1: Pickup in Chicago. Crew arrives 8 AM, loads through the day, on the road by late afternoon.
  • Days 2–3: Transit. Driver checks in daily.
  • Day 4 or 5: Delivery in Tampa. Unload, placement, reassembly. Done by evening.

Total: 4–5 days door to door. Binding quote: ~$6,200.

Your specific timeline depends on pickup date, delivery city, and route conditions.

FAQ

How is a binding quote different from a non-binding estimate?

A binding quote locks in price, weight allowance, and delivery window. It doesn’t change between booking and delivery. Most long-distance movers give non-binding estimates that go up at delivery. Ours doesn’t.

Will my stuff be on a shared truck?

No. Every Sparkle long-distance move uses a dedicated truck. Nothing else on it.

How long does Chicago to Florida actually take?

3–5 days transit. South Florida (Miami, Naples) is the longer end at 4–5 days. North Florida (Jacksonville, Tallahassee) is shorter at 3 days. Pickup and delivery each take a full day.

Do you handle reverse moves (Florida to Chicago)?

Yes. Same model — dedicated truck, binding quote, real delivery window. Quoted from the Florida pickup ZIP.

Can I track the truck during transit?

Yes. The driver checks in daily. You can call or text the crew lead anytime during the route.

What if my dates change?

Seven days notice or more: no fee. Less than seven days: depends on truck availability and route.

Is everything insured during the long drive?

Standard liability coverage is included. Higher-value coverage is available for high-value items, quoted with the move. See Insurance & Licensing →.

Do you do partial loads or shared trucks for cheaper rates?

No. We don’t co-mingle loads. The dedicated-truck model is what makes the delivery window real.
