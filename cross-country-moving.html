Frequently asked questions

How is cross-country different from interstate moving?

Both cross state lines. Cross-country specifically means longer hauls, typically 1,500+ miles, coast-to-coast or near it. Interstate covers any state-line move, near or far. Same crew, same truck, same binding quote either way.

How long does a cross-country move take?

5–8 days for coast-to-coast routes. 3–5 days for closer cross-country destinations like the East Coast or Florida. Your binding quote shows the specific window.

Will I have the same crew at both ends?

Most of the time, yes. The team that loads in Chicago unloads at the destination. On the longest routes (West Coast, Pacific Northwest), the driver may meet the destination crew at delivery. Either way, you have one point of contact start to finish.

What if I need to store some items at the destination?

We can hold your shipment at the destination for up to 30 days, billed monthly. See Storage Services.

Do I need to be there at pickup and delivery?

You or someone you authorize. Doesn’t have to be the same person at both ends. The driver coordinates timing the day before each.

Is there an extra fee for stairs or long carries at delivery?

No. Stairs and standard carries are included.

What if I add or remove items between booking and pickup?

Inventory adjustments at booking are free. Adjustments after the binding quote is signed are quoted upfront. No surprises at the truck.
