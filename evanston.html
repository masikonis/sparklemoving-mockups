Local logistics

What it takes to move in Evanston

A few neighborhood details we already handle, so you don’t have to.

  • Buildings and COIs.

    Most Evanston multi-unit buildings require a certificate of insurance to use the freight elevator. We file the COI directly with the building before move day.

  • Permit parking.

    Many residential streets in Evanston are sticker-permit zones. We check the rules for your block and arrange permitted parking when needed, so the truck has a legal spot the day of.

  • Northwestern moves.

    Move-in week in late August and move-out in mid-June fill up fast. Book three weeks ahead for those dates if possible. Same-day university moves are doable when crew is available.

  • Lakefront high-rises.

    Sherman Plaza, the Optima buildings, and similar high-rises run on scheduled elevator slots and tight loading docks. We coordinate the slot with the building, not the customer.

  • One-way streets and tight blocks.

    Central Evanston has narrow side streets and several one-ways. The crew does a route check before dispatch so the truck arrives the right way.

Frequently asked questions

Do you charge a zone fee for Evanston?

No. Same hourly rate as the rest of the metro. The travel fee covers drive time, nothing else.

How long does an Evanston-to-Chicago move take?

A 1-bedroom typically runs 2–4 hours. A 2-bedroom runs 4–6 hours. A 3-bedroom house runs 6–10 hours. The drive itself is usually 30–45 minutes each way.

Can you handle a same-day move in Evanston?

Often yes, depending on crew availability. See Last Minute Moving.

What about Northwestern dorm or off-campus moves?

Yes. We work weekends during move-in and move-out weeks. Book three weeks ahead in late August and mid-June if possible.

Do you serve all Evanston neighborhoods?

Yes. Downtown, the lakefront, North Evanston, Central Street, west of Ridge, all of it.

Where do trucks dispatch from?

Northbrook, IL. About ten miles north of Evanston.
