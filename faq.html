Booking and quotes

How do I get a quote?

Enter your ZIPs and a few move details on the Start a Quote form. Takes about 60 seconds. We send the breakdown by email within a few hours.

How far in advance should I book?

Local moves: 1–2 weeks for a typical job. Same-day and next-day jobs are doable when crew is available. Long-distance moves: 2–4 weeks ahead. Peak season (May through August) and end-of-month dates fill up faster.

Do you require a deposit?

No. You pay in full at the end of the move, once everything is placed.

Is my quote binding?

For local moves, you get an hour range and a fixed hourly rate. The final number reflects actual time worked, within that range. For long-distance moves, you get a binding quote — total cost, weight allowance, and delivery window all locked in.

Can you handle a same-day or next-day move?

Often yes, depending on crew availability. See Last Minute Moving.

Pricing

What’s the hourly rate?

$139/hr for 2 movers, $169/hr for 3 movers, $199/hr for 4 movers. Rates are for the full crew, not per mover. Same rate every day of the week.

What’s the minimum?

Two hours for a 2-mover crew. Three hours for 3- or 4-mover crews. Plus the one-hour travel fee.

What does the travel fee cover?

One flat charge equal to one hour of crew time. Covers drive time from our warehouse to your pickup and back from your destination. Billed once, not each way.

Are there hidden fees?

No. Full fees and add-ons on the Pricing page. Stairs are free up to the 2nd floor. Standard furniture disassembly and reassembly is free. No fuel surcharge, no weekend premium, no after-hours premium.

What if my move runs longer than estimated?

You’re billed at the same hourly rate for actual time worked. No overtime multiplier. If the crew finishes early, you’re billed for actual hours only.

Do you charge for stairs?

Not up to the 2nd floor. Higher walk-ups are quoted in your estimate before you book.

How does pricing work for long-distance moves?

Long-distance is a binding quote — total cost, weight allowance, delivery window, all fixed at booking. Pricing depends on weight, distance, and truck space. See Long Distance Moving.

Day of move

When does the crew arrive?

Within a one-hour window on move day. The crew lead calls ahead with an exact ETA.

Will I have the same crew the whole move?

Yes. The team that loads is the team that unloads.

Do you wrap and protect furniture?

Yes. Wood and upholstered pieces get blanket-wrapped. Mattresses and soft goods get shrink-wrapped. Floor protection goes down at both addresses.

Do you disassemble and reassemble furniture?

Yes. Beds, tables, dressers, and flat-pack furniture come apart and go back together at no extra charge. Hardware goes in a labeled bag, taped to the piece.

Do I need to be there the whole time?

Yes for the start (walkthrough) and the end (placement and walkthrough). Middle of the day, you don’t need to ride in the truck.

What if my building requires a certificate of insurance?

Send us the building’s COI requirements when you book. We file the COI and confirm with the building before move day.

Long distance

How long does a long-distance move take?

Florida and East Coast: 3–5 days. Texas: 2–4 days. West Coast and Pacific Northwest: 5–8 days. Your quote shows the specific delivery window.

Will my stuff be on a shared truck?

No. Every Sparkle long-distance move uses a dedicated truck. Nothing else on it.

Can I track the truck during transit?

Yes. We send updates at pickup completion, mid-transit, and on the day of delivery. The crew lead is reachable directly the whole route.

What states do you serve?

Anywhere in the lower 48. Hawaii, Alaska, and overseas moves are quoted separately.

Storage

Do you offer storage between move-out and move-in?

Yes. Up to 30 days of storage during a move is included free in our Northbrook warehouse.

What about longer-term storage?

Monthly storage starts at $95/month for a small unit. Climate-controlled units are available for an additional fee. See Storage Services.

Where is the warehouse?

Northbrook, IL. The same facility our trucks dispatch from.

Is the storage facility climate-controlled?

Climate-controlled units are available. Standard units are not. Ask at the quote stage and we’ll note the right unit type for your items.

Insurance and damages

Are you licensed and insured?

Yes. Full license numbers and insurance details on the Insurance & Licensing page.

What does basic liability coverage include?

Coverage of $0.60 per pound per item, the federal minimum for licensed movers. Higher-value coverage is available for an additional fee, quoted with the move.

What if something gets damaged?

Document the damage and let us know within 14 days. Claim process is on the Insurance & Licensing page.

Can you provide a certificate of insurance for my building?

Yes. Send us the building’s requirements when you book. We file the COI directly.

Cancellations and changes

Can I change my move date?

Yes. Seven days notice or more: no fee. Less than seven days: depends on crew availability and route.

Can I cancel?

Yes, no fee. Since we don’t take a deposit, there’s nothing to refund.

Can I add stops to the move?

Yes. Extra stops are billed at the same hourly rate — they’re just more crew time. Tell us during the quote so we can plan the route.

Tipping and payment

How do I pay?

Cash, check, or credit card. No processing fee on cards.

Is tipping expected?

Tips are appreciated, never assumed. Common range is $20–$40 per mover for a typical local move.

Do you accept business accounts?

Yes. For commercial moves and recurring office work, we set up direct billing with payment terms. See Commercial Moving.
