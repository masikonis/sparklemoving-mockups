Released value

What basic liability actually covers.

Federal regulation requires every licensed interstate mover to provide a minimum coverage of $0.60 per pound per item for damages in transit. It’s free, included in every move, and nominal — a 50-pound TV worth $1,000 is covered for $30 under basic liability.

We follow the same minimum on local moves. It’s the floor, not the recommendation.

Full-value protection

Higher-value coverage.

For high-value items — electronics, art, antiques, anything you’d file an actual claim on — we offer full-value protection. If your move includes anything you’d hate to replace at the basic-liability rate, ask at the quote stage.

  • How it works.

    You declare a value at booking. If an item is lost, damaged, or destroyed, we either repair it, replace it, or pay you the declared value.

  • Cost.

    Quoted with the move. Depends on declared value and any high-value items called out separately (jewelry, art, collectibles).

Building paperwork

Certificates of insurance for buildings.

Most multi-unit and commercial buildings require a certificate of insurance from the moving company before allowing the freight elevator and dock to be used. This is included — no charge for filing the COI, no upcharge for buildings that require unusual limits.

  • What you do.

    Send us the building’s COI requirements when you book. They usually come from the building manager as a one-page sheet of required limits and named insureds.

  • What we do.

    File the COI with our insurance carrier and confirm directly with the building before move day. You don’t make the call.

If something gets damaged

Damage claim process

We’d rather resolve a claim than fight it. The process is the proof.

  1. 1

    Note it on move day if possible.

    The crew lead documents it on the final invoice. Photos help.

  2. 2

    Submit the claim within 14 days.

    Email claims@sparklemoving.com with the move date, the item, the damage, and photos. A claim form goes back to you within 2 business days.

  3. 3

    Inspection and resolution.

    For small claims, we inspect by photo and resolve directly. For larger claims, we schedule an in-person inspection. Resolution is repair, replacement, or payout — your choice within the policy.

  4. 4

    Timeline.

    Most claims resolve within 30 days of submission. Larger claims can take longer if a third-party repair quote is needed.

Frequently asked questions

Are you registered with the FMCSA?

Yes. We hold both a USDOT number and an MC number for interstate moves. Both are listed above and verifiable on the federal registry.

Are you registered with the Illinois Commerce Commission?

Yes. ICC MC number listed above for intrastate (within Illinois) moves.

What’s the difference between USDOT and MC numbers?

A USDOT number is required for any commercial vehicle operating in interstate commerce. An MC number is the operating authority that lets a mover transport household goods across state lines for hire. Most legitimate interstate movers carry both. We do.

Are your trucks owned or leased?

Owned. We don’t subcontract the move out to a different fleet.

Are crew members on payroll or contracted?

On payroll. The crew that arrives is on Sparkle’s payroll and covered by our workers compensation policy.

Do you offer Released Value Protection or Full-Value Protection?

Both. Released Value (basic liability at $0.60/lb) is included free. Full-Value Protection is the upgrade — quoted at booking based on declared value.

Where do I file a complaint if it’s not resolved with you directly?

For interstate moves, the FMCSA accepts complaints at the FMCSA complaint portal. For Illinois intrastate moves, the ICC accepts complaints at the ICC complaint portal. We’d rather you call us first, but the public process is there.
