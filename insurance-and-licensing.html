What basic liability covers

Federal regulation requires every licensed interstate mover to provide a minimum coverage of $0.60 per pound per item for damages in transit. It’s free, included in every move, and nominal — a 50-pound TV worth $1,000 is covered for $30 under basic liability.

We follow the same minimum on local moves. It’s the floor, not the recommendation.

Higher-value coverage

For high-value items (electronics, art, antiques, anything you’d file an actual claim on), we offer full-value protection.

How it works. You declare a value at booking. If an item is lost, damaged, or destroyed, we either repair it, replace it, or pay you the declared value.

Cost. Quoted with the move. Depends on declared value and any high-value items called out separately (jewelry, art, collectibles).

If your move includes anything you’d hate to replace at the basic-liability rate, ask about full-value protection at the quote stage.

Certificates of insurance for buildings

Most multi-unit and commercial buildings require a certificate of insurance from the moving company before allowing the freight elevator and dock to be used.

What you do: Send us the building’s COI requirements when you book. They usually come from the building manager as a one-page sheet of required limits and named insureds.

What we do: File the COI with our insurance carrier and confirm directly with the building before move day. You don’t make the call.

This is included. No charge for filing the COI, no upcharge for buildings that require unusual limits.

Damage claim process

If something is damaged during the move, here’s the path:

  1. Note it on move day if possible. The crew lead documents it on the final invoice. Photos help.
  2. Submit the claim within 14 days. Email claims@sparklemoving.com with the move date, the item, the damage, and photos. A claim form goes back to you within 2 business days.
  3. Inspection and resolution. For small claims, we inspect by photo and resolve directly. For larger claims, we schedule an in-person inspection. Resolution is repair, replacement, or payout — your choice within the policy.
  4. Timeline. Most claims resolve within 30 days of submission. Larger claims can take longer if a third-party repair quote is needed.

We’d rather resolve a claim than fight it. The process is the proof.

Verify our credentials yourself

You don’t have to take our word for any of this.

Federal (interstate moves). Search our USDOT or MC number on the FMCSA SAFER registry →. You’ll see registration status, fleet size, inspection history, and any out-of-service orders.

Illinois (intrastate moves). Search our ICC MC number on the Illinois Commerce Commission registry →. Status, certificate, and any complaints filed are public record.

Insurance certificates. Available on request. Email insurance@sparklemoving.com and we’ll send the current COI for your records.

FAQ

Are you registered with the FMCSA?

Yes. We hold both a USDOT number and an MC number for interstate moves. Both are listed above and verifiable on the federal registry.

Are you registered with the Illinois Commerce Commission?

Yes. ICC MC number listed above for intrastate (within Illinois) moves.

What’s the difference between USDOT and MC numbers?

A USDOT number is required for any commercial vehicle operating in interstate commerce. An MC number is the operating authority that lets a mover transport household goods across state lines for hire. Most legitimate interstate movers carry both. We do.

Are your trucks owned or leased?

Owned. We don’t subcontract the move out to a different fleet.

Are crew members on payroll or contracted?

On payroll. The crew that arrives is on Sparkle’s payroll and covered by our workers compensation policy.

Do you offer Released Value Protection or Full-Value Protection?

Both. Released Value (basic liability at $0.60/lb) is included free. Full-Value Protection is the upgrade — quoted at booking based on declared value.

Where do I file a complaint if it’s not resolved with you directly?

For interstate moves, the FMCSA accepts complaints at FMCSA complaint portal →. For Illinois intrastate moves, the ICC accepts complaints at ICC complaint portal →. We’d rather you call us first, but the public process is there.
