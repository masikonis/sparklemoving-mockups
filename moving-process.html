1. Quote

Enter your ZIPs and a few details: home size, type of move, date. About 60 seconds.

For local moves, you get a breakdown: crew size, hourly rate, minimum, and an hour range. For long-distance moves, you get a binding quote — total cost, weight allowance, and delivery window all locked in.

If anything’s missing or the move’s unusual (piano, tight access, freight elevator, two-leg trip), we’ll send a couple of follow-up questions before quoting.

2. Booking

You confirm the date and pay nothing upfront. No deposit.

Our office sends the booking confirmation, the quote, and a list of anything we need from you: building requirements, COI requests, parking notes.

3. Pre-move week

Building paperwork is on us. We file the COI with your building, book the freight elevator slot, and pull the parking permit if your block needs one. You don’t call the building manager.

If your dates change, seven days notice or more is no fee. Less than seven days depends on crew availability.

4. Crew arrival

The crew arrives within a one-hour window on move day. The crew lead calls ahead with an exact ETA. Same crew that’s on the booking, no last-minute substitution.

5. Walkthrough

Ten minutes, before anything moves. The crew lead walks the home with you, confirms what’s going and what’s staying, and notes anything fragile or oversized.

Floor protection goes down. Door jambs and rails get padded. Then loading starts — the hourly clock starts here.

6. Loading

Furniture wrapped in blankets. Mattresses and soft goods shrink-wrapped. Boxes loaded in stacks, heaviest at the bottom.

Disassembly is included. Beds, tables, dressers, flat-pack furniture come apart and the hardware goes in a labeled bag, taped to the piece.

You don’t lift anything. If something needs a decision (which couch goes first, what stays), the crew lead asks.

7. Transit

The crew drives. You don’t ride in the truck — meet us at the destination on your own schedule.

For local moves, transit is part of the hourly rate. For long-distance, transit is included in the binding quote, with a delivery window that doesn’t move.

8. Unload and placement

Reverse of loading. Floor protection, then placement. The crew lead walks the new place with you and confirms where each piece goes.

Reassembly is included. Beds get put back together, tables go back upright, dressers get drawers reset.

We don’t leave until you’ve checked the truck. Final walkthrough catches anything missed.

9. Final invoice

The crew lead writes up the final invoice on site. Hours worked, travel fee, any add-ons (boxes, supplies). You pay in full at the end — cash, check, or card. No processing fee on cards.

If the crew finishes early, you’re billed for actual hours only. If the move runs long, you’re billed at the same hourly rate. No overtime multiplier.

The hourly clock — where it starts and stops

The clock starts when the crew arrives at your pickup and is ready to load. It stops when the last item is placed at the destination and the walkthrough is done.

On the clock

  • Loading at pickup
  • Drive time between addresses
  • Unloading and placement at destination
  • Disassembly and reassembly of standard furniture

Not on the clock

  • The drive from our warehouse to your pickup
  • The drive from your destination back to our warehouse

Both are covered by the one-time travel fee, equal to one hour of crew time.

Long-distance moves don’t run on a clock. The binding quote covers everything end to end.

FAQ

How long does a typical move take?

1-bedroom: 2–4 hours, 2-mover crew. 2-bedroom: 4–6 hours, 3-mover crew. 3-bedroom: 6–10 hours, 4-mover crew. Your quote shows the estimate for your specific move.

Do I need to be there the whole time?

Yes for the start (walkthrough) and the end (placement and walkthrough). Middle of the day, you don’t need to ride in the truck.

Do you wrap and protect everything?

Yes. Wood and upholstered furniture get blanket-wrapped. Mattresses and soft goods get shrink-wrapped. Floor protection goes down at both ends.

What if I find damage after the crew leaves?

Document it and let us know within 14 days. Claims process is on the Insurance & Licensing page →.

Can I add stops to the move?

Yes. Extra stops are billed at the same hourly rate — they’re just more crew time. Tell us during the quote so we can plan the route.

What about pianos, safes, and oversized items?

Quoted separately so you see the cost upfront. See Additional Services →.
